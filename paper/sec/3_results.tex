\paragraph{Accuracy} Results shown in Figure~\ref{fig:teaser} (left) shows that standard normalized DINOv3 embeddings without any feature preprocessing achieve a performance \textcolor{red}{$\sim 30\%$} of mAcc, Taking in account feature preprocessing unlocks more than $\sim 20\%$ of improvement.   

\paragraph{Feature Preprocessing} The FungiTastic dataset is characterized by substantial nuisance variability, including changes in background, illumination, zoom, acquisition conditions, and broader domain shift. These factors dominate the geometry of DINOv3 CLS-token embeddings, masking directions that are more informative for fine-grained fungi classification. As shown in \ref{fig:teaser} (right), neither raw-feature normalization in the original $4096$-dimensional space nor vanilla PCA is sufficient to effectively distinguish classes, revealing the mismatch between the intrinsic geometry of the embedding space and the class-discriminative metric required for prototype comparison. The discriminative class structure emerges more clearly after PCA whitening, i.e. after projecting the features onto the principal-component basis and rescaling each retained component by its standard deviation. The resulting decorrelated and variance balanced representation makes cosine similarity less influenced by nuisance class-independent and high-variance directions, allowing lower and medium-variance class-specific components to contribute more effectively to class separation.

\paragraph{Completed segmentation slice: DINOv3 + MLP.}
\Cref{tab:mlp-results} retains the completed slice that already exists in the repo. The main pattern is consistent across both resolutions: \texttt{cls+masked} is the best configuration, outperforming plain \texttt{cls} by 1.82 points at 224 and 1.23 points at 448. Mask-aware pooling alone stays close to \texttt{cls} at 224 but falls behind at 448, while plain patch averaging is clearly weak. In the latest 224 rerun, the exploratory register variants also trail \texttt{cls+masked}, so the strongest current evidence still favors global context plus segmented local evidence.
