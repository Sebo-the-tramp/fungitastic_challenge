\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{What the completed evidence already supports.}
Even after broadening the paper, the current DINOv3 slice still says something concrete. Segmentation helps most when it refines local evidence without replacing the global representation. Both the MLP results and the 224 $k$-NN sweep prefer \texttt{cls+masked} over \texttt{patch}, and neither supports a patch-only summary as a strong default.

\paragraph{Limits and next steps.}
The broader manuscript asks questions about segmentation regimes, backbone families, fusion diversity, and classifier choice, but the current evidence covers only one backbone and an incomplete classifier set. The local claim that mask-aware fusion helps is therefore supported only for the current DINOv3 setting. The next concrete steps are to export the 2000-class SAM-3 teaser, run the cross-family backbone sweep, finish the prototype and linear-probe baselines, and then add stratified analyses by class frequency, mask area, and hard species pairs.
