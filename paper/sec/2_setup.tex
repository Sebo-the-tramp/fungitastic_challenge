\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Dataset and evaluation protocol.}
We evaluate our approach on the FungiTastic dataset \cite{Picek_2025_CVPR}, a fine-grained benchmark characterized by subtle inter-class differences and a highly imbalanced, long-tailed distribution. The dataset contains approximately 13k images with segmentation annotations (Fig.~\ref{fig:fungitastic}) spanning over 200 differen classes.

To ensure consistent evaluation, we restrict the label space to 194 classes present in both training and test splits. We report results in one-shot, few-shot, and full-data regimes. Unless otherwise stated, evaluation is performed on the full test set without rebalancing.

\paragraph{Segmentation setup.}
We use SAM3 to extract object masks. For prompt-based baselines, we evaluate both coarse and fine-grained textual prompts, applying thresholds of 0.5 and 0.3 respectively. 

Prompt-based SAM requires one forward pass per class and image, resulting in segmentation cost that grows linearly with the number of classes. In contrast, our method performs a single class-agnostic SAM pass per image and assigns labels in a separate stage, making segmentation cost constant with respect to the label space (Fig.~X).

\paragraph{Backbone models.}
Our primary backbone is DINOv3 \cite{simeoni2025dinov3}, used as a frozen feature extractor. We additionally evaluate DINOv2 \cite{oquab2023dinov2}, CLIP \cite{radford2021learningtransferablevisualmodels}, \SCc{and masked autoencoder variants to assess the impact of representation quality.}

\paragraph{Classification.}
We extract global image representations from frozen backbones and train lightweight classifiers on top. Unless otherwise stated, we use the \texttt{[CLS]} token as the feature representation. We also explore alternative representations, including register tokens and mean-pooled patch embeddings.

\paragraph{Segment-based augmentations.}
Leveraging the available segmentation masks, we construct augmented views by modifying the background while preserving the foreground object. Specifically, we (i) mask the background and (ii) apply background blurring. These augmentations are used to enrich feature representations in low-data regimes.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
We report top-1 and top-5 classification accuracy, as well as mean IoU for segmentation. Additionally, we introduce classification-aware IoU (CaIoU), defined as:
\[
\mathrm{CaIoU} = \mathrm{IoU}(\hat{M}, M) \cdot \mathbf{1}[\hat{y} = y],
\]
which measures segmentation quality conditioned on correct classification.
