\begin{abstract}
This draft aligns the paper with the experiment plan stated in the repo README. The study is organized around three axes for fine-grained fungal recognition on FungiTastic \cite{Picek_2022_WACV,picek2022automatic,picek2024fungitastic}: segmentation strategy, frozen visual backbone, and classifier choice. The intended top-line story is summarized by two teaser comparisons: a 200-class setting with oracle masks and a 2000-class setting with SAM-3 masks, both comparing the DINOv3 suite with and without segmentation-aware features under MLP classification. The broader ablations then compare CLIP, SigLIP, MAE, DINOv1, DINOv2, DINOv3, and InternViT backbones, together with prototype, $k$-NN, linear-probe, and MLP classifiers. At the current repo state, completed evidence covers the first DINOv3 slice only: MLP token variants at 224 and 448, plus a 224 $k$-NN sweep. In this completed slice, \texttt{cls+masked} is the strongest MLP representation, reaching \textbf{74.04}\% at 224 and \textbf{75.53}\% at 448, while the best 224 $k$-NN result is \texttt{cls+masked@k=10} at \textbf{52.86}\%. The manuscript is therefore structured around the final README-defined story, but it keeps placeholder teaser figures and explicitly marks the parts that are still pending.
\end{abstract}
